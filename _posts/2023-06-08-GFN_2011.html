This Global Financial Network was created based on the sum of portfolio, direct, and inter-banking investment flows between countries. The raw data was derived from locational banking statistics(BIS) and coordinated portfolio and direct investment statistics(IMF). This map only contains financial flows of which gross flows (the sum of asset and liability flows) is over $100 trillion. The size of a circle represents the amount of stock values, which is the total amount of net financial flows between a country and the rest of the world. For example, the stock of the USA represents the total assets minus the total liabilities, which the USA hold to the rest of the world. Moreover, the color of a circle is designated by its modularity class, representing the community in which a country is affiliated. Countries within a community has closer relationship than the countries included in the other communities. Finally, the width of a line shows the amount of assets, liabilities, and gross flows between the two countries. Each tab shows different financial flows.
